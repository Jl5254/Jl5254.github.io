The Ultimate Three Layer Cake!

It can be difficult to choose one flavour of cake, so why not have them all?

Follow this simple recipe and you will have an eye-catching centerpiece fit for any tea party!

Preperation time: 40-45 minutes

Serves 12

Adapted from BBC Good Food

Last posted: 12-09-24

Ingredients

Lemon Sponge:

  • 110g unsalted butter

  • 110g caster sugar

  • 2 large eggs

  • 110g self-raising flour

  • 0.5 tsp baking powder

  • 37g natural yogurt

  • 0.5 tsp vanilla extract

  • 3 zested and juiced lemons

  • 75g granulated sugar

Chocolate Fudge Sponge:

  • 88g self raising flour

  • 1 tbsp cocoa powder

  • 0.5tsp bicarbonate soda

  • 75g caster sugar

  • 1 egg

  • 75 ml sunflower oil

  • 75 ml semi-skimmed milk

  • 1 tbsp golden syrup

Coffee and Walnut Sponge:

  • 125g unsalted butter

  • 50 ml strong black coffee with 1tbsp coffee granules

  • 140g self-raising flour

  • 125g golden caster sugar

  • 0.25 tsp baking powder

  • 2 eggs

  • 0.5tsp vanilla extract

  • 40g walnut, finely chopped

Buttercream Icing:

  • 600g icing sugar

  • 300g unsalted butter

Method

  1. Heat the oven to 180C/ 160C fan/ gas mark 4 and grease three 20cm tins with unsalted butter. Line these tins with baking paper.

  2. For the lemon sponge, with an electric whisk, beat the butter and sugar together until the mixture has a smooth consistancy.

  3. Beat each egg into the mixture, and then fold the flour and baking powder into the mixture.

  4. Fold in the yogurt, vanilla extract and half the lemon zest until the mixture is fully combined and pour into one of the tins.

  5. For the chocolate fudge sponge, sieve the flour, cocoa and bicarbonate of soda into one bowl and mix with the sugar.

  6. In a well of the dried ingredients, add the golden syrup, beaten egg, oil and milk and beat with an electric whisk. This batter can then be placed into a second tin.

  7. For the final coffee and walnut sponge, beat the butter, flour, sugar, baking powder, eggs, vanilla and half of the coffee with an electric wisk.

  8. Fold in the chopped walnuts and add into a tin.

  9. Bake all three mixtures for 25-30 minutes, making sure to place the lemon cake on the top shelf as it may take 30-35 minutes.

  10. As the sponges cool, beat the sifted icing sugar and butter together with 2-3 tbsp of warm water.

  11. When the sponges are cool, assemble the three layers with icing in between sponges, on the top and around the sides.

  12. Garnish with sprinkles, berries or decorations of your choice and enjoy!
